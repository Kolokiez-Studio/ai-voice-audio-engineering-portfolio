# Voice Prep Checklist

This checklist outlines how I evaluate and prepare spoken voice material for voice production, dubbing, cleanup, AI voice workflows and client-ready delivery.

---

## 1. Recording Quality

Before editing or processing, I check whether the raw voice material is technically usable and emotionally representative.

Key points:

- Clean signal without clipping
- Low and consistent noise floor
- Minimal room reflections
- No heavy background noise
- Consistent microphone distance
- No excessive compression or reverb printed into the recording
- Clear articulation
- Natural performance
- Stable vocal tone
- Enough usable clean material for the intended use case

A technically clean recording is not enough on its own. The voice also needs to represent the intended character, energy, language, pacing and emotional direction.

---

## 2. Voice Consistency

For voice-driven production, consistency is critical.

I listen for:

- tonal consistency across takes
- stable distance to the microphone
- consistent loudness and energy
- consistent emotional delivery
- matching room tone
- changes in fatigue or vocal tension
- pronunciation shifts
- sudden changes in brightness, proximity or texture

Even small inconsistencies can become distracting when a voice is used across longer passages, dubbing work or AI voice production.

---

## 3. Editing Preparation

Before any final production step, I clean and organize the material carefully.

Typical editing checks:

- Remove obvious clicks, pops and interruptions
- Control plosives without making the voice dull
- Reduce mouth clicks carefully
- Preserve natural breath and phrasing where appropriate
- Remove distracting noises between phrases
- Avoid over-editing that makes speech unnatural
- Check transitions between edited sections
- Maintain consistent loudness between takes
- Keep the performance emotionally intact

The goal is not to make the voice sterile. The goal is to make it clean, natural and usable.

---

## 4. AI Voice / Voice Cloning Suitability

When preparing voice material for AI voice workflows, I ask:

- Is the source audio clean enough?
- Is the speaker’s voice well represented?
- Is the performance natural and not exaggerated?
- Are there enough clean sections?
- Is the room tone controlled?
- Are there artifacts that could affect downstream processing?
- Is the audio over-processed?
- Does the voice remain consistent across the material?
- Are pronunciation and articulation clear?
- Would this source audio help or hurt the final output?

A voice model can only learn from what is present in the source material. Bad input usually creates limitations later in the process.

---

## 5. Final Listening Pass

Before delivery or further production, I do a final listening pass in context.

I check:

- Is every word understandable?
- Does the voice feel natural?
- Are there any remaining clicks, pops or edit artifacts?
- Is the emotional tone intact?
- Does the loudness feel consistent?
- Does the voice fit the intended use case?
- Does the material sound professional on headphones and speakers?
- Would I be comfortable sending this to a client?

My standard is simple: the voice should feel clear, human, intentional and ready for the next production step.
